This Privacy Policy explains how Judgment Collection Service collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you visit judgementcollectionservice.com or submit information through the site.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect information you provide directly, including:

  • name, email address, phone number, and contact details
  • judgment details, case information, court information, and related documents
  • messages, questions, or other information you send through forms or email

We may also collect technical information automatically, such as IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, referral source, and general usage data.

2. How We Use Information

We may use information to:

  • review and evaluate submitted judgment matters
  • respond to inquiries and communicate with you
  • determine whether a matter may qualify for referral or follow-up
  • operate, maintain, secure, and improve the website
  • monitor website performance, traffic, and abuse prevention
  • comply with legal obligations and protect our rights

3. How We Share Information

We may share information with service providers, contractors, hosting vendors, form providers, analytics providers, and independent recovery professionals or law firms when a submission may be appropriate for referral or further review.

We may also disclose information when required by law, to respond to legal process, to investigate misuse, or to protect rights, property, or safety.

4. Cookies and Analytics

We or our third-party tools may use cookies, pixels, log files, and similar technologies to understand website activity, improve site performance, and support basic analytics or security functions.

You can usually control cookies through your browser settings, although disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

5. Third-Party Services

This website may rely on third-party services such as hosted forms, analytics platforms, embedded content, and website infrastructure providers. Those third parties may collect or process information according to their own privacy policies, which we do not control.

6. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

We may retain submitted information for as long as reasonably necessary to review matters, support follow-up, maintain records, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

8. Your Choices

You may contact us to request updates or deletion of information you submitted, subject to legal, operational, or recordkeeping requirements. We may need to retain certain information where required or permitted by law.

9. Children’s Privacy

This website is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

10. Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a Do Not Track setting. Because there is no uniform standard for responding to these signals, this website may not respond to Do Not Track requests.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time by posting a revised version on this page. Your continued use of the website after changes are posted means you accept the updated policy.

12. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, contact us through the submission process on the main website.
